SkillBench: A Hierarchical and Scalable Benchmark for Fine-grained Compositional Text-to-Image Generation

Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Despite significant advances in text-to-image (T2I) models, current approaches still struggle to effectively combine multiple concepts described by compositional textual prompts into a coherent scene. Developing benchmarks to systematically analyze the compositional capabilities of T2I models is challenging yet crucial. Existing benchmarks either lack comprehensive coverage of compositional skills or fail to provide fine-grained definitions of these skills, limiting the ability to conduct in-depth, multigranularity evaluations. To address these issues, we introduce \(\textbf{SkillBench}\), a hierarchical and scalable benchmark to evaluate compositional T2I generation. Specifically, we define compositional skills in a \(\textbf{fine-grained}\) and \(\textbf{hierarchical}\) manner. These skills are categorized into primitive skills and composite skills. Furthermore, each skill is evaluated across varying difficulty levels to evaluate how well T2I models generalize from simpler to more complex tasks. Additionally, we propose \(\textbf{FITScore}\), a Fine-grained, Interpretable, and Training-free evaluation framework tailored for compositional T2I generation. By integrating structured analysis with interpretability, FITScore facilitates automatic and transparent assessment of T2I alignment, enhancing adaptability to diverse scenarios and ensuring robust evaluation performance in open-world settings. Through an evaluation of nine recent large-scale T2I models, our analysis provides valuable insights into the strengths and limitations of current models when addressing fine-grained compositional skills.
